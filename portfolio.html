This is a render of a donut I made using Blender. I created the bun using sculpting and texturepainting. I add the icing with more sculpting and added different kinds of sprinkles on top

Facts:
- 500 sprinkle particles
- Took about 20 minutes to render

This is a render of a kitchen. With this project I tried creating an environment, like a indoor room instead of singular objects. I used some textures, the bowl and the artwork in the back from a website called Poliigon

Facts:
- Rendered in 2048 samples.
- Took 90 minutes to render.

This is a render of a towel. It consists of a plane, with a lot of vertices. I then made the material cloth and simulated it, so it fell in a realistic way. I then added lots of tiny hairs, to give it a realistic and nice look.

Facts:
- The towel has 150.000 hairs.
- It took 45 minutes to render.

This is a render of a cup of coffee I made using Blender. I created a cup and a plate, then I created the liquid inside of the cup and gave it a coffee texture by UV-unwrapping it.

Facts:
- The fluid inside of the cup is simulated.
- If you look closely, you can see the condensation.

This is a render of a choclate donut and a tea cup I made using fluid simulation, particle systems and texture painting.

Facts:
- About 700 sprinkle particles.
- Took about 70 minutes to render.
- Used 1024 samples

This is a render of a rope, consisting of smaller ropes bundled together. The rope was made using bump mapping. You can also see the sea, which is simulated water thrown at the shore to create movement and waves.

Facts:
- The sea is simulated water with a real water shader.
- The rope consists of smaller ropes.
- The rope was formed using a bezier curve.
- Two lamps were used for the shine of the water, and the shadow behind the rope.

This is a render of a bedroom. It's one of my more recent projects, in which I used the denoise node in Blender. This is a new feature and it is a really intelligent system of removing noise. Together with a lot of samples, this created a really clear image.

Facts:
- The blanket is real simulated cloth.
- I added a little bit of bloom to make lights pop a little bit more.
- I used 2048 samples and the denoise feature to make it really clear
- The render lasted almost two hours, the blanket and rug alone took about 45 minutes.

This is a render of planet earth I made. It is a simple UV Sphere, together with another sphere for the clouds and atmosphere. I used four different maps from NASA to create the material of earth. I then made the sun and atmosphere, and in the compiler I did some post-processing to create this scifi image.

Facts:
- The sun is just some post-processing effects combined with an emission material.
- I used only 1 light-bounce, because it is in space.
- The scene has only four objects, so 32 samples were enough to create a clear image.

This is a render of a 1980's looking apartment in a skyscraper. It is a isometric render, which is a flat picture, Which doesn't show depth, even though in the scene there is depth. I tried to create a more basic scene, with basic shapes and geometry, and a low poly count.

Facts:
- There are only 43.100 faces, which is a relatively low amount.
- I used hybrid rendering, which is a new feature in Blender 2.8. It let's you render with both CPU and GPU, for faster render times.
- Because the scene was pretty simple, and to try out the new hybrid rendering feature, I used 1024 samples, and still the render took only about 8 minutes.

This is a isometric render of a student bedroom. The bedsheets were simulated using the cloth simulation and the pillows were sculpted using the built-in sculpting mode. It was rendered in cycles in 4K using 1024 samples.

Facts:
- I used images for the desktop, the printer, the clock and the poster, which reduced render times a bit.
- The render took about 45 minutes.
- The scene consists of a lot of basic geomatric shapes, even though it looks organic.

This is an animation of a sci-fi themed earth rotating a full 360 degrees. It was rendered using cycles in 4K, however it is shown in Full HD.

Facts:
- I used a relatively low sample count, because it actually is a very basic scenes. It consists of only 3 spheres for earth, and 1 sphere for the sun.
- Because the sample amount was quite low, the part that actually took the longest, was post-processing and compositing.
- I used 360 frames, at 30 frames per second, to create a 12 second long animation in 1080p.

This is a 4K render of the new Porsche Taycan Turbo, in my opinion a beautifull car. I rendered it using cycles and a HDRI you can find here: HDRIHaven: Ostrich Road

Facts:
- The scene consists of two things: A plane which represents the ground, and the car itself.
- I used an HDRI to create the environment, the plane is there to use for a technic called a 'Shadow Catcher', which is an invisible plane, which is used by other objects to cast shadows.
- Creating cars requires different techniques, because a car has lots of sharp creases and edges, while still having a organic flow. It was hard to make the car look organic, while still having sharp edges.
